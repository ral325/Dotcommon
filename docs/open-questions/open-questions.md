# Open Questions Register

Questions that remain unresolved and require future investigation, external input, or governance decisions.

---

## OQ-001: USPTO trademark status for "Dotcommon"

**Priority:** High — must be resolved before any public launch or brand investment  
**Context:** "Dotcommon" appears unclaimed in the financial services trademark namespace based on informal search, but a formal USPTO search has not been conducted. The dot-com component may face pushback as generic, though "common" transforms it into a semantically distinct compound.  
**Action required:** Formal USPTO search at tmsearch.uspto.gov, class 36 (financial services). Consider engaging a trademark attorney given the strategic importance of the name.

## OQ-002: Cooperative legal entity formation

**Priority:** High — prerequisite for approaching CDFI funders including Seed Commons  
**Context:** Dotcommon needs formal cooperative legal entity formation before it can operate, receive funding, or enter member agreements. Jurisdiction affects this significantly — California has strong cooperative law; other states vary.  
**Action required:** Identify cooperative attorney, select jurisdiction, file articles of incorporation as a worker cooperative or multi-stakeholder cooperative.

## OQ-003: Colony 3% network fee — negotiability at scale

**Priority:** Medium  
**Context:** Colony charges ~3% on all expenditure payouts as a network fee. At $80k member income with full platform routing, this is $2,400/member/year — by far the largest single overhead line item. At scale this becomes a significant cost that erodes the cooperative advantage.  
**Action required:** Investigate whether Colony offers volume pricing, enterprise agreements, or fee waivers for non-profit or cooperative use cases. Evaluate at what membership scale it becomes worth building governance infrastructure in-house.

## OQ-004: TLSNotary production readiness timeline

**Priority:** Medium  
**Context:** TLSNotary is the recommended long-term income attestation protocol but is currently in research/early production stage with rough developer experience. The v1 plan uses Reclaim Protocol as a bridge, but the migration timeline is uncertain.  
**Action required:** Monitor TLSNotary's PSE roadmap. Establish a migration trigger criteria (e.g. "migrate when TLSNotary has production SDK with >6 months stability and documentation comparable to Reclaim").

## OQ-005: Money transmission licensing exposure

**Priority:** High  
**Context:** Operating a mutual credit network may trigger money transmission licensing requirements in certain jurisdictions. The cooperative structure and member-owned nature of the pool may qualify for exemptions, but this is unverified.  
**Action required:** Legal review by attorney with fintech/cooperative law experience. Identify which states require licensing, what exemptions apply to member-owned cooperatives, and whether the CDFI pathway provides regulatory cover.

## OQ-006: Optimism sequencer centralisation risk

**Priority:** Low-Medium  
**Context:** Optimism's sequencer is currently centralised, meaning a single operator (Optimism Foundation / OP Labs) could theoretically censor transactions or go offline. Decentralisation is on the roadmap but not complete.  
**Action required:** Monitor Optimism decentralisation progress. Establish a contingency plan for migrating to a different L2 if sequencer centralisation becomes a material risk. The DAI contract itself is chain-agnostic.

## OQ-007: Founding member governance transition protocol

**Priority:** Medium  
**Context:** Colony's bootstrapping mechanism assigns initial reputation to founding members equal to token allocation. This creates a temporary governance advantage that decays over 18–24 months. The exact transition point from founding-team governance to full member governance needs to be specified in founding documents.  
**Action required:** Draft constitutional language specifying: (1) maximum founding reputation cap, (2) trigger conditions for governance transition (e.g. "when founding team holds less than 33% of total reputation"), (3) how to handle founding team members who remain active and maintain high reputation legitimately.

## OQ-008: Pool liquidity reserve requirements

**Priority:** Medium  
**Context:** The credit pool must hold capital against simultaneous draws. The cost of holding these reserves (opportunity cost or explicit reserve funding) is not currently in the cost model.  
**Action required:** Model liquidity reserve requirements as a function of membership, average credit utilisation, and draw timing correlation. Add to cost model once estimated.
