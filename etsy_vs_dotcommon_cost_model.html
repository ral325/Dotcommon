ETSY FEE CALCULATIONS
Listing fees are $0.20 per initial listing plus $0.20 per renewal. Renewals occur every 4 months on unsold items and automatically on each sale. The model calculates unsold renewals as listings × floor(12 / listAgeMo / 4).

Transaction fee is 6.5% of sale price including any shipping charge collected. Modelled on GMV directly — assumes shipping is passed through at cost so the fee applies to the item price component.

Payment processing is 3% + $0.25 per transaction via Etsy Payments, which is mandatory in most countries. The per-transaction count is derived from GMV ÷ average sale price.

Offsite Ads is 15% of attributed sales for sellers under $10k/yr GMV (opt-out available) and 12% for sellers over $10k/yr (mandatory). The model applies the appropriate rate to GMV × OA attribution percentage.

Refund credit: Etsy refunds the transaction and payment processing portion of fees on returned orders but not the listing fee. Modelled as 9.5% of refunded GMV.

Shipping label discount estimated at $2.40 per transaction, representing approximately 30% off a typical $8 retail label. Shown as a benefit (cost offset) in the Etsy column.
DOTCOMMON CALCULATIONS
Membership fee is a flat annual amount covering all platform infrastructure. No listing fee, no transaction commission.

L2 gas is estimated at $0.02 per transaction (escrow contract execution) plus $0.005 per listing (IPFS content hash anchoring). At typical L2 prices these are upper-bound estimates.

Fiat ramp applies double conversion — inbound (client fiat to stablecoin) and outbound (stablecoin to seller fiat) — at the selected rate. Sellers who invoice in stablecoin or hold a portion of earnings in stablecoin pay toward the lower end.

Tax admin software ($228/yr, TaxJar entry tier) is a differential cost — Etsy handles sales tax collection and remittance automatically. Shown as a Dotcommon cost only when the toggle is enabled. Etsy's automatic remittance is conservatively valued at $150/yr in the Etsy benefits column.

Independent ad spend to replace Offsite Ads traffic defaults to $8 per OA-attributed transaction. This reflects warm retargeting and comparison shopping traffic (not cold acquisition, which would be $30–$40/sale). The slider allows adjustment from $1 to $40 per sale to reflect different seller contexts and ad channel efficiency.

Participation stake dividend: surplusPool = (netSize × membershipFee) − fixedCosts − (varCost × netSize). dividendPerStake = surplusPool / totalNetworkStakes. Fixed operating costs do not scale with members, so the surplus pool grows faster than the network as membership increases — this is the mechanism by which dividendPerStake rises over time. In the 5-year projection, netSize compounds at the selected growth rate, totalNetworkStakes compounds at 60% of that rate (new members start with zero stakes and accumulate them gradually), and surplusPool grows faster than totalStakes because fixedCosts are amortised across more members each year. Faster network growth therefore produces a higher dividendPerStake in later years.
WHAT IS NOT MODELLED
Organic Etsy traffic: Etsy's 90M+ buyer base provides discovery that Dotcommon cannot replicate at launch. This model does not quantify the revenue impact of reduced discoverability — it only models cost differences on equivalent GMV.

Seller time costs: The administrative burden of tax compliance, independent marketing, and platform management is not quantified. Both platforms have non-zero time costs that vary by seller.

Etsy advertising spend: Many sellers run Etsy Ads (on-platform promoted listings) separately from Offsite Ads. This optional cost is not included — it would increase the Etsy cost further.

Currency conversion: International sellers face additional fees not modelled here.

Seller growth: The cost vs GMV chart assumes the seller's profile (listings, avg price, OA%) stays constant as GMV scales, which is a simplification.
5-YEAR PROJECTION ASSUMPTIONS
Seller GMV growth: Seller's own GMV assumed to grow at 5%/year regardless of scenario. The scenarios vary the Dotcommon network growth rate, not the seller's revenue.

Network growth rates: Conservative 20%/yr, moderate 40%/yr, optimistic 70%/yr compound annual growth in network membership starting from 1,000 members.

Dividend per stake: Grows logarithmically with network size, reflecting the increasing surplus generated by a larger membership fee pool and growing network value reserve. Conservative estimate — real dividend growth depends on transaction volume and governance decisions.

Etsy fees: Assumed constant at current rates. Etsy has raised fees several times in recent years; any future increases would widen the saving further.

Dotcommon membership fee: Assumed constant. A mature cooperative might reduce fees as operating costs amortise across a larger member base, which would widen the saving further.
key equations
ETSY COST MODEL
transactions
transactions = floor( GMV / avgSalePrice )
listing fees
renewalsPerListing = floor( 12 / listAgeMo / 4 )
listingFees = $0.20 × ( listings × (1 + renewalsPerListing) + transactions )
transaction fee
txFee = GMV × 0.065
payment processing
paymentFee = GMV × 0.03 + transactions × $0.25
offsite ads
oaRate = GMV < $10k ? 15% : 12%
oaFee = GMV × oaPct × oaRate
refund credit
refundCredit = GMV × refundRate × 0.095
total etsy cost
etsyCost = listingFees + txFee + paymentFee
          + oaFee + subscriptions
          − refundCredit − shippingDiscount − taxRemitValue
DOTCOMMON COST MODEL
payment infrastructure
gasFee = transactions × $0.02 + listings × $0.005
rampFee = GMV × rampRate × 2   ← double conversion
network surplus pool
feeRevenue = netSize × membershipFee
operatingCosts = fixedCosts + varCost × netSize
surplusPool = max( 0, feeRevenue − operatingCosts )
stake accrual
stakesPerYear = GMV × STAKES_PER_DOLLAR + govVotes × STAKES_PER_GOV_VOTE
half-life decay
annualDecayRate = 1 − 0.5^(1 / halfLifeYears)
effectiveStakes = stakesPerYear × (1 − (1−decayRate)^tenure) / decayRate
derived totals
totalNetworkStakes = netSize × effectiveStakes
sellerHeldStakes = effectiveStakes ← seller = avg member
dividend per stake & seller dividend
dividendPerStake = surplusPool / totalNetworkStakes
sellerDividend = surplusPool × (sellerHeldStakes / totalNetworkStakes)
total dotcommon cost
coopCost = membershipFee + gasFee + rampFee
          + taxAdminFee + adSpend
          − sellerDividend
5-YEAR PROJECTION
network & stakes evolution
netSize[y] = netSize[y-1] × (1 + growthRate)
newStakes[y] = netSize[y] × stakesPerYear
totalStakes[y] = totalStakes[y-1] × (1−decayRate) + newStakes[y]
Decay erodes old stakes each year; new issuance from growing membership replenishes them.
seller stake evolution
sellerStakes[y] = sellerStakes[y-1] × (1−decayRate) + sellerGMV[y] × STAKES_PER_DOLLAR
GMV[y] = GMV × 1.05^(y-1)
Seller's old stakes decay but GMV growth means they earn more new stakes each year — ownership fraction rises if GMV grows faster than the network.
why dividendPerStake grows
surplusPool[y] = netSize[y] × fee − fixedCosts − varCost×netSize[y]
dividendPerStake[y] = surplusPool[y] / totalStakes[y]
fixedCosts are constant — surplusPool grows with netSize while totalStakes is bounded by decay, so dividendPerStake rises as the network scales.
